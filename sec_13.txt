14. Risk Assessment & Mitigation
14.1 Overview
Every software solution introduces technical and operational risks that should be identified during the design phase. Evaluating these risks early enables the team to define appropriate mitigation strategies and reduces the likelihood of unexpected issues during deployment or production.

14.3 Technical Risks
Several technical risks are associated with AI-powered chatbot systems.
Retrieval Accuracy
If the retrieved website content does not closely match the user's question, response quality may decrease.
Mitigation:
Semantic embeddings.
Optimized chunking strategy.
Similarity threshold tuning.
Continuous evaluation using analytics.

Knowledge Freshness
Outdated website content may result in inaccurate responses.
Mitigation:
Event-driven incremental synchronization.
Metadata versioning.
Synchronization history tracking.

AI Provider Dependency
Reliance on a single LLM provider may introduce operational risks.
Mitigation:
Provider-independent AI abstraction.
Support for future integration with additional providers.

14.4 Operational Risks
Operational risks relate to deployment, infrastructure, and ongoing maintenance.
Potential concerns include:
Server downtime.
Database connectivity issues.
Docker container failures.
Misconfigured environment variables.
Failed knowledge synchronization.
Mitigation measures include:
Health monitoring.
Container restart policies.
Backup procedures.
Deployment validation checklists.

14.5 Business Risks
Business-related risks should also be considered.
Examples include:
Users expecting answers beyond the chatbot's supported domain.
Changes to business services without corresponding website updates.
Increased operational costs due to higher AI usage.
Mitigation:
Clearly define the chatbot's scope.
Keep the website as the single source of truth.
Monitor usage trends and API costs through the Operational Dashboard.

14.6 Risk Review Process
Risks should be reviewed periodically during development and after production deployment.
The review process should include:
Analysis of operational metrics.
User feedback.
Failed or unanswered queries.
AI response quality.
Knowledge base coverage.
Continuous review allows the chatbot to evolve while maintaining reliability and accuracy.
